01 — The Mark

Icon & Logo.

The Escalion mark is a directional D-pad glyph in white, set on a halftone-dot field that fades from signal orange into black. It reads as a game controller and an editor cursor at once. Always use the mark with its rounded-square (22% radius) container. Do not extract the glyph on its own for primary use.

Primary · on black
On light surface
On orange surface

Clear space

Keep a minimum margin of 40% of the icon height clear on all sides. Nothing — text, edges, or other logos — should intrude on this zone. Minimum display size is 24 px square.

✓ Do
  • Keep the rounded-square container intact
  • Use on deep black, orange, or off-white surfaces
  • Preserve the drop shadow / glow on dark backgrounds
  • Scale proportionally — never stretch
✕ Don't
  • Recolor the glyph or the container
  • Rotate, skew, or add extra effects
  • Place on busy photos without a scrim
  • Crop the corners or square off the radius

04 — Language

Design Elements.

The recurring building blocks. Layer these — halftone under content, scanlines over everything, glow behind the mark — to keep every surface unmistakably Escalion.

Halftone field
Orange dot grid, 28px pitch, masked to fade toward the edges. The signature texture.
Scanlines
1px dark lines every 4px, laid over the whole frame for a CRT / terminal feel.
Orange glow
Radial bloom behind the mark and headline words. Warmth and focus.
Corner ticks
Bracketing marks that frame the icon like a reticle / viewfinder.
Clipped button
Solid orange CTA with notched top-right / bottom-left corners.
Mono tag
Tracked monospace pill on a faint orange wash — for tech / metadata.
